# Scout Voice & Tone Guide

## Team Personality

We're nerdy and technical but love teaching and welcoming people in. Not gatekeepers—we want people to succeed. Thoughtful builders who ship fast. Honest about challenges. Action-oriented with a "consider it done" mentality. Friendly and approachable with a wicked sense of humor.

This personality shows in our content, especially blogs, educational materials, and community engagement. We're not trying to sound corporate—we're Scout, and we have a distinct point of view.

---

## Tone Principles

### 1. Direct Without Jargon

- Plain language for complex concepts
- Be specific: "automate your sales workflows" not "revolutionize your go-to-market strategy"
- Focus on outcomes: "Your best sales reps are spending 40% of their time on admin work instead of selling"

### 2. Confident but Humble

- Show confidence through what we've built, not claims
- Acknowledge challenges openly
- Never oversell or overpromise

### 3. Action-Oriented

- Lead with what people can do
- Active voice, strong verbs
- "Consider it done" energy

### 4. Educational & Accessible

- We teach, we don't gatekeep
- Break down complex topics
- Make AI adoption feel achievable

### 5. Problem-Focused

- Start with the real problem: "The RFP time sink that's killing your sales velocity"
- Show you understand the pain before presenting the solution

## Language

### Use:

- Action: "Build" / "Ship" / "Deploy" / "Automate" / "Launch"
- Simplicity: "Let Scout handle the tech lift" / "Choose presets or go custom"
- Outcomes: "Less building, more doing"
- Support: "White-glove support" (we actually provide this)
- Specificity: "Workflows" not "processes" / "AI agents" not "AI solutions"

### Avoid:

- Corporate buzzwords: "synergy," "leverage," "paradigm shift," "revolutionize"
- Passive voice when active works better
- Hype without substance: "game-changing" (unless you can prove it)
- Pretending hard things are easy
- Gatekeeping technical knowledge

### Scout Terms:

- Agents - AI assistants that take action across your tools
- Workflows - Automated task sequences that run on schedule or on-demand
- Databases - Where you store data for your AI to reference ("your AI's personal library")
- Collections - What Databases used to be called
- Tables - A table in a Database (Databases have multiple tables)
- White-glove support - We connect to your data, build the agents, and help you launch

## Content Specifics

### Homepage/Product Pages:

- Lead with the problem, then the solution
- Clear value proposition
- Specific use cases and outcomes
- Direct CTA

### Blog Posts:

- Leverage narrative storytelling when possible to draw the reader in
- Mix tactical and strategic
- Teach something valuable, even if they don't use Scout

### Changelog:

- Lead with the theme: "Less building, more doing"
- Explain the why, not just the what
- Be conversational

### Educational Content (University, Webinars):

- Welcoming and accessible
- Step-by-step, practical
- Context-rich explanations

### Documentation:

- Precise and complete
- Example-rich
- Conversational: "Think of it as building your AI's personal library"
- Consider agent-readability

## Examples

### Homepage:

No: "We're thrilled to announce our revolutionary new AI-powered solution..."
Yes: "Let Scout handle the tech lift. We connect to your data, build AI agents, then launch them into the tools you already use. Choose presets or go custom, all with white-glove support."

### Problem Statement:

No: "Organizations face challenges with RFP completion..."
Yes: "The RFP time sink that's killing your sales velocity. Your best sales reps are spending 40% of their time on admin work instead of selling."

### Feature Description:

No: "Our platform leverages cutting-edge LLMs to facilitate seamless integration..."
Yes: "Scout agents connect to your existing tools—Salesforce, HubSpot, Slack, whatever you use. They automate the repetitive work your team shouldn't be doing manually."

### Changelog:No: "We have implemented several enhancements to improve user experience..."
Yes: "Less building, more doing. We're making Scout easier to use. A redesigned interface that gets out of your way."

### Educational:

No: "To initialize a collection, navigate to..."
Yes: "Collections are where you store documents, data, and information that your AI can search through. Think of it as building your AI's personal library."

## Final Principles

1. Lead with the problem - Show you understand before you solve
2. Be useful - Every piece of content should help someone do something
3. Be honest - About capabilities, limitations, and complexity
4. Be specific - Vague claims don't help anyone
5. Be welcoming - Teach, don't gatekeep
6. Be clear - Simple language, direct communication
7. Be action-oriented - Focus on what people can do
8. Pay attention to structure - Make content easy to skim, use headers, lists, images where relevant

---

*When in doubt, ask: "Is this useful? Is it honest? Is it clear? Does it help someone get their work done? Does it sound like us?"*
