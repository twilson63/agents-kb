# Blog Voice & Style Guide

## Base Style
Follow AP Style as the default standard. Key rules:

### Numbers
- Spell out one through nine; use numerals for 10 and above
- Always use numerals for ages, percentages, and measurements
- Never start a sentence with a numeral — rewrite the sentence

### Dates & Times
- Abbreviate months with 6+ letters when paired with a date: Jan., Feb., Aug., Sept., Oct., Nov., Dec.
- Never abbreviate: March, April, May, June, July
- No ordinal suffixes: Jan. 5, not Jan. 5th
- Time format: 3 p.m., 10:30 a.m.

### Punctuation
- No Oxford comma: red, white and blue
- No em dashes — ever. Rewrite the sentence, use a comma, or use a colon instead
- Periods and commas go inside quotation marks
- Avoid semicolons; restructure the sentence instead
- Use parentheses sparingly

### Capitalization
- Capitalize formal titles before a name, lowercase after: President Biden, Joe Biden, the president
- Seasons are lowercase: spring, fall

### Abbreviations & Acronyms
- Spell out on first reference, then abbreviate: Federal Bureau of Investigation (FBI)
- No periods in most acronyms: NATO, FBI
- Use periods in two-letter country/region abbreviations: U.S., U.K.

### Percentages & Money
- Always use the % symbol with numerals: 5%
- Use $ with numerals: $4, $2.5 million

---

## Voice & Tone

Write like a person with a point of view, not a content generator. Every post should have a clear argument or insight, not just a summary of what could be said on the topic.

## AI Writing Tropes to Avoid

### Banned Phrases
- "Delve into"
- "In today's fast-paced world..."
- "Game-changer" / "paradigm shift"
- "Tapestry" (when describing complex topics)
- "Nuanced" (as a filler word)
- "Navigating the challenges of..."
- "Crucial" / "Essential" / "Vital" (used for generic emphasis)
- "It's worth noting that..."
- "It's important to remember..."
- "Exciting developments are happening"

### Structural Tropes to Avoid
- Do not restate the question before answering it
- Do not use rhetorical questions as transitions ("But what does this mean for you?")
- Do not pad conclusions by restating everything already covered
- Do not bullet-point content that reads better as prose
- Do not present fake balance ("both sides") without committing to a view
- Do not cite vague authority: "experts say..." or "studies show..." requires a specific source

### Tone Rules
- Take a stance. Avoid corporate neutrality
- Do not over-hedge every statement
- No sycophantic transitions
- Helpful and specific beats comprehensive and vague
