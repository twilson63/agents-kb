# AP Style Guidelines

*Reference guide based on the Associated Press Stylebook*

The AP Stylebook is the standard reference for news writing, used by newspapers, magazines, and communications professionals. Its core principles are:

- Consistency
- Clarity
- Accuracy
- Brevity

---

## Numbers

- Spell out one through nine; use figures for 10 and above
- Never begin a sentence with a figure (except calendar years)
- Use figures with the word "percent" (e.g., 2 percent, not 2%)
- Spell out "million" and "billion" with figures (e.g., $8 million)
- Money: Use numerals, spell out cents/million/billion (e.g., $26.52, 6 cents)

### Ordinals
- Spell out first through ninth
- Use figures for 10th and above

---

## Dates and Times

### Dates
- Use numerals for days without st, nd, rd, th (e.g., March 12, not March 12th)
- Abbreviate: Jan., Feb., Aug., Sept., Oct., Nov., Dec. when used with a date
- Spell out: March, April, May, June, July when used with a date
- No comma between month and year alone (e.g., October 1937)
- Use commas to set off year with full date (e.g., May 6, 1869, was...)

### Times
- Use lowercase a.m. and p.m. with periods
- Use figures with a space (e.g., 6:30 a.m.)
- No :00 for exact hours (e.g., 1 p.m., not 1:00 p.m.)
- Spell out noon and midnight
- Time ranges: 6:30-10 p.m. (same period) or 10 a.m.-2 p.m. (spans both)

### Decades
- Use figures with s, no apostrophe (e.g., 1990s, not 1990's)
- Use apostrophe before figures if numerals omitted (e.g., '90s)

---

## Punctuation

### Commas
- No Oxford comma in simple series (e.g., red, yellow and blue)
- Use comma before terminal conjunction only in complex series with internal conjunctions
- Use comma to set off a person's residence or age

### Hyphens
- Hyphenate compound adjectives before a noun for clarity (e.g., fastest-growing company)
- Do not hyphenate with -ly adverbs (e.g., newly formed company)
- Do not hyphenate compound modifiers after the noun
- Ages as adjectives: a 21-year-old student, the 18-year-olds

### Dashes
- Use to set off a series, indicate a break in thought, or attribute a quotation
- Use two hyphens (--) if em dash unavailable

### Quotation Marks
- Periods and commas go inside quotation marks
- Colons go outside quotation marks (unless part of quoted material)

### Apostrophes
- Possessives: Add 's for all singular nouns (e.g., boss's, James's)
- Exception: Singular proper names ending in s take only apostrophe (e.g., Kansas')
- No apostrophe for decades (1990s) or acronyms (CDs)

### Other
- Single space after a period
- Avoid parentheses; use dashes or commas instead

---

## Capitalization and Titles

### Personal Titles
- Capitalize formal titles before a name (e.g., President Bush, Dr. Smith)
- Lowercase titles after a name or set off by commas (e.g., Evan Bayh, a senator from Indiana)
- Abbreviate: Dr., Gov., Rep., Rev., Sen. before full name (first reference)

### Courtesy Titles
- Do not use Mr., Mrs., Miss, Ms. unless in a direct quote or needed to differentiate

### Works and Compositions
- Use quotation marks for titles of books, songs, TV shows, poems, works of art
- Do not use quotation marks for magazines, newspapers, the Bible, or reference books

---

## Names and References

- First and last name on first reference
- Last name only on subsequent references
- Roman numerals for wars (World War II) and sequences (Pope John Paul II)

---

## Abbreviations and Acronyms

- Acceptable without explanation: FBI, CIA, GOP
- Avoid "alphabet soup" - don't overuse acronyms
- Spell out state names in body text
- Abbreviate states with city names (e.g., Cambridge, Mass.)
- Never abbreviate: Alaska, Hawaii, Idaho, Iowa, Maine, Ohio, Texas, Utah

---

## Addresses

- Use figures for numbered addresses
- Abbreviate Ave., Blvd., St. and directional cues with a number (e.g., 101 N. Grant St.)
- Spell out other words (alley, drive, road)
- Spell out street names First through Ninth; figures for 10th and higher

---

## Technology Terms

- email (one word, no hyphen)
- website (one word, lowercase)
- internet (lowercase)
- smartphone (one word)
- Other e-words are hyphenated (e.g., e-reader, e-commerce)

---

## Phone Numbers

- Use hyphens between groups (e.g., 555-123-4567)
- Do not precede with "1" or a plus sign

---

## Dimensions and Measurements

- Use figures and spell out words (e.g., She is 5-foot-3)
- Figures for distances over 10; spell out for 10 or less

---

## Ages

- Always use figures
- Hyphenate as adjective (a 21-year-old student) or noun substitute (for 18-year-olds)
- No apostrophes for age ranges (in his 20s)
